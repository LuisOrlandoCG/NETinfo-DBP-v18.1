NETinfo DBP Version 18.1 : Remote Cheque Deposit - Mobile app

Created by Fivi Konstantinou, last modified by 1216-Mappoura Andrea on 11, 11, 2024

The Remote Cheque Deposit solution enables end-users to deposit their cheques through their mobile app without visiting a local branch. The ‘Remote Cheque Deposit’ request from the end-user may be viewed and processed through the NETinfo Admin Console.

NOTE: The Mobile app pages are implemented within the scope of the Project; the NETinfo DBP product only provides the required APIs

Product Main Features / Business Requirements

  1. The Remote Cheque Deposit function conforms to the standard three step process: Submit, Confirm, Complete

  2. For successful processing, the end-user must:

    1. Select a Credit Account

    2. Enter the Amount to be deposited

    3. Take a picture of the Front and Back side of the cheque

  3. Allowed credit accounts are:

    1. Accounts related to a bank product that has the Credit Flag set in the ‘Bank Products’ admin page.

    2. Accounts that end-user has full access rights as defined in User Profile.

    3. Accounts under the Customer selected by end-user from the ‘Owner’ field

    4. Accounts of eligible currencies (as configured through the ‘Transaction Type Currency’ page in admin console)

  4. The eligible currencies involved in a deposit are configurable.

  5. SCA functionality is not supported.

  6. Daily limits at customer, account, user and user-account level are not enforced.

  7. Minimum and Maximum Transaction Limits can be enforced (configurable).

  8. Remote Cheque Deposit requests may be accessed for further processing by authorised admin-users (through the NETinfo Admin Console)

  9. A dedicated Alert (the ‘Remote Cheque Deposit’ alert) is provided for informing end-user on changes on the status of their deposit requests

  10. The following features are not supported:

    1. Signatures

    2. Calculation of charges

    3. OCR

    4. Cross currency transfers (i.e. Credit Account and Amount to be deposited having different currencies)

Activity Diagram

Figure below depicts the end-user workflow when submitting a Remote Cheque Deposit requests. Referring to this, end-user follows the following steps:

  • Selects the account to which the cheque will be deposited

  • Enters the amount (and currency) to be deposited

  • Takes a picture of the Front Side of the Cheque; if not satisfied with quality, end-user may select to retake the picture

  • Takes a picture of the Back Side of the Cheque; if not satisfied with quality, end-user may select to retake the picture

  • Confirms the request after confirming that the information associated with the request is correct

About the Activity Diagram: The Activity Diagram depicts the main activities in the workflow: boxes indicate end-user activity, decision boxes indicate system decision points and arrow text in brackets indicate major system activity/actions.

End-User is navigated through the following pages to ensure that all required information (for processing the request) are captured:

  • Select Account page

  • Scan Images pages

Scan Image Pages

The figure below (UI/UX), presents the ‘Scan Image’ input pages. Referring to this, end-user is prompted to read the instructions provided by the bank and scan the front side of the cheque. In case they are satisfied with the quality of the image captured, they may proceed with capturing the back-end side of the cheque by selecting the ‘Use Photo’ option and pressing ‘Proceed’ on the pop up message that appears; alternatively, they may select the ‘Retake’ option for retaking the picture of the cheque.

The same process is followed for capturing the image of the back-side of the cheque. When end-user is satisfied with the quality of the image captured, then by pressing the ‘Use Photo’ option a confirmation pop-up appears; upon selecting ‘Done’, they are navigated to the Confirm Page.

The following actions are taken by the system:

  • The transaction is submitted to the administrator for manual processing (Remote Cheque Deposit Processing)

  • The status changes to ‘Submitted’ (or ‘Failed’ if something goes wrong)

  • The transaction is registered to the ‘Online Transaction Status’

  • The ‘Remote Cheque Deposit’ alert is forwarded to the end-user

  • The transaction is logged
