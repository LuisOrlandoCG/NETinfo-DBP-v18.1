NETinfo DBP Version 18.1 : Mass Authorisations

Created by 1057-Michael Philippos, last modified on 14, 11, 2024

When a mass payment linked to an approval workflow is initiated from any of the channels connected to the NETinfo DBP, the assigned approvers (i.e. signatories or checkers) receive an alert notifying them of the event. Approvers can subsequently view the details of the mass payment and approve or decline mass payment transactions (one by one or all at once) as required.

Product Main Features / Business Requirements

  1. Authorised approvers (i.e. signatories or checkers) can view the details of a mass payment (along with details of individual transactions within it) that is pending for approval on them, and approve or decline payment transactions, one by one or all at once, as required (partial processing of mass payment is not allowed)

  2. Mass payment authorisations is a three step process (Submit, Confirm, Complete)

  3. End-users receive an alert (the ‘Mass Authorisation’ alert - configurable) when a mass payment is pending authorisation on them. Upon login:

    1. A notification is displayed in dashboard informing approver of pending mass payments for approval

    2. They may visit the ‘Mass Authorisations’ notification centre to view all mass payments pending approval on them

  4. For each mass payment pending approval the following information is displayed:

    1. Created On i.e. date on which mass payment has been submitted

    2. File Name i.e. the name of the physical file (containing the payment instructions) that has been submitted

    3. From Account i.e. the debit account

    4. Total Amount i.e. this is the total equivalent amount in debit account currency

  5. Ability to:

    1. Retrieve mass payments based on debit account, number of last transactions & period

    2. Filter retrieved entries using keyword search and by amount

    3. Export information in EXCEL or PDF format

  6. Ability to view (in a modal) and export (in PDF or EXCEL format) additional details on a mass payment as below, and approve/decline payments, one by one or all at once:

    1. Information relating to the mass payment transaction e.g. status, reference number, mass payment details and approval related information

    2. Information on individual fund transfer transactions with expand/collapse option e.g. transaction details and status

    3. Totals (number of transactions and amounts) on debit account currency equivalent and per currency

  7. Alerts are forwarded to inputter and next approver as applicable upon successful processing (by a signatory) of a mass payment

  8. In case of final approver, all system checks & actions are performed as in ‘Mass Payments’ page

  9. SCA functionality is supported (configurable)

  10. Disclaimers for the input and confirm pages are supported (configurable)

Activity Diagram

Figure below depicts the workflow of the end-user when accessing the ‘Mass Authorisations’ page. Referring to this, end-user is first presented with the last 10 mass payments, from all their accounts, that are pending authorisation; for each mass payment the following information is displayed:

  • Created On i.e. date on which mass payment has been submitted

  • File Name i.e. the name of the physical file (containing the payment instructions) that has been submitted

  • From Account i.e. the debit account

  • Total Amount i.e. this is the total equivalent amount in debit account currency (declined transactions not included)

End-user has the option to:

  • Change the period of interest (i.e. last 10/20/30 transactions or user defined period) or debit account

  • Filter the records retrieved using keyword search and/or by amount

  • Export the transactions retrieved in PDF or EXCEL format

  • View further details on an individual mass payment by ‘clicking’ on the associated record; this opens a modal displaying:

    • Information on the mass payment transaction e.g. status, reference number, mass payment details and approval related information

    • Details on individual fund transfer transactions with expand/collapse option e.g. transaction details and status

    • Totals (number of transactions and amounts) on debit account currency equivalent and per currency

    • Options to:

      • Select one by one the payment transactions to approve or decline

      • Select all payment transactions to approve or decline

    • Option to export in PDF or EXCEL

Upon processing all transactions within the mass payment (i.e. either approve or decline them), end-user is navigated to the ‘Confirm’ page (after the necessary system checks are performed) where they are prompted to view the details of the transactions they are about to submit for further processing/execution; Upon pressing the ‘Confirm’ button the following actions take place:

  1. In case this is the final approval and mass payment is to be executed immediately then:

    1. System performs same checks as with ‘Mass Payments’, including Limit and Credit Check validations (these are also performed upon pressing the ‘Submit’ button)

    2. In case SCA is configured, then end-user is forced to go through the SCA workflow

    3. Mass payment transactions are scheduled for execution as individual records based on the setting of the ‘Pending Batches’ timer; status changes to ‘Pending’ and ‘Processed’ after actual submission to Core System

    4. Alert is forwarded to inputter informing them of the change is status

    5. The ‘Complete’ page is displayed

  2. In case this is the final approval and mass payment is to be executed in a future date then:

    1. Certain system checks are performed (e.g. transaction type limits, field validations)

    2. In case SCA is configured, then end-user is forced to go through the SCA workflow

    3. Status changes to ‘Pending’

    4. Alert is forwarded to inputter informing them of the change is status

    5. The ‘Complete’ page is displayed

  3. In case this is not the final approval then:

    1. Certain system checks are performed (e.g. transaction type limits, field validations)

    2. In case SCA is configured, then end-user is forced to go through the SCA workflow

    3. Status remains as ‘Pending for Authorisation’

    4. Alert is forwarded to next approver(s) as applicable

    5. The ‘Complete’ page is displayed

About the Activity Diagram: The Activity Diagram depicts the main activities in the workflow: boxes indicate end-user activity, decision boxes indicate system decision points and arrow text in brackets indicate major system activity/actions.

Mass Authorisations: Main Page

The figure below (UI/UX - Mass Authorisations: Main Page), presents the ‘Mass Authorisations' main page. Referring to this, when end-user first opens the page, the last 10 mass payments pending authorisation, from all their accounts, are displayed by default; end-user may change this, by selecting the option of interest from the ‘Products’ and ‘Period’ fields (in case of ‘User Defined’ selection, end-user is prompted to enter the ‘From’ and ‘To’ dates and then press the ‘Go’ button).

All mass payments matching the search criteria are displayed in the <Mass Payments> area. End-user can further filter the transactions retrieved through keyword search or by amount range.

For each mass payment displayed, end-user can view the following information:

  • Created On date: This is the date the mass payment was submitted

  • File Name: this is the name of the file as uploaded in the ‘Mass Payments’ page

  • From Account: This is the debit account as as provided in the ‘Mass Payments’ page

  • Total Amount: Displays the total equivalent amount (in debit account currency; declined transactions are not included)

End-user may:

  • Select to see further details of and process a mass payment by clicking on the associated record - this opens the ‘Mass Payment Details Modal'

  • Export the mass payments displayed by selecting the ‘Export’ option and subsequently selecting the preferred report format (PDF or EXCEL)

A disclaimer may appear at the bottom of the page if configured.

Mass Payment Details Modal

he figure below (UI/UX - Mass Payment Details Modal), presents the modal displayed when end-user ‘clicks’ on a mass payment record (from the <Mass Payments> area). Referring to this, end-user may:

  • View certain key information mainly relating to the details and processing of the payment file

  • View the individual fund transfer instructions within the mass payment file and their associated status

  • May expand an individual fund transfer instruction to view additional information

  • View the Totals (i.e. total number of transactions and amount in debit account equivalent currency with option to expand to view totals per currency; declined transactions are not included in calculations)

  • Export the information on this page (excluding the information displayed when expanding an individual payment instruction) in PDF or EXCEL format

End-user may:

  • Approve or decline individual payment transactions by selecting the corresponding action and then pressing the ‘Submit’ button

  • Reset the approvals/declines on individual transactions by pressing the ‘Clear’ button

  • Approve or decline all transactions by pressing the ‘Approve All’ and ‘Decline All’ buttons respectively and then pressing the ‘Submit’ button

End-user can close the dialogue box at any time by pressing the ‘Close’ icon

    1. System performs same checks as with ‘Mass Payments’ submission, including Limit and Credit Check validations (these are also performed upon pressing the ‘Submit’ button)

    2. In case SCA is configured, then end-user is forced to go through the SCA workflow

    3. Fund transfer transactions are submitted for execution as individual records based on the setting of the ‘Pending Batches’ timer; status changes to ‘Pending’ and ‘Processed’ after actual submission

    4. Alert is forwarded to inputter

    5. The ‘Complete’ page is displayed

  2. In case this is the final approval and mass payment is to be executed in a future date then:

    1. Certain system checks are performed (e.g. transaction type limits, field validations)

    2. In case SCA is configured, then end-user is forced to go through the SCA workflow

    3. Status changes to ‘Pending’

    4. Alert is forwarded to inputter

    5. The ‘Complete’ page is displayed

  3. In case this is not the final approval then:

    1. Certain system checks are performed (e.g. transaction type limits, field validations)

    2. In case SCA is configured, then end-user is forced to go through the SCA workflow

    3. Status remains as ‘Pending for Authorisation’

    4. Alert is forwarded to next approver(s) as applicable

    5. The ‘Complete’ page is displayed
