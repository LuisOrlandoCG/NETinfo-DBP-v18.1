Transfer Between Own Accounts

Fund Transfer to Other Bank Customers

End-users can initiate a money transfer  between their ‘connected accounts’.  The payment instruction can be submitted (to the Core System) for execution in real time or at a future date or as a standing order.  

Know More >>>

End-users can initiate a money transfer (future payments are also supported) from one of their own accounts (or from an account they have the relevant privileges) to a beneficiary within the bank. End-users can do this by entering the beneficiary account number or by selecting a registered beneficiary (which stores the beneficiary account details). The payment instruction is submitted in real time and executed by the Core System.

Transfer to Local Banks

SEPA Transfers

End-users can initiate a money transfer (future payments are also supported) from one of their own accounts (or from an account they have the relevant privileges) to a beneficiary held within another bank. End-users can do this by entering the beneficiary account details or by selecting a registered beneficiary (which stores the beneficiary account details). The payment is submitted in real time and executed by the Core System.

Know More >>>

End-users can initiate a SEPA money transfer (future payments are also supported) from one of their own accounts (or from an account they have the relevant privileges) to a beneficiary held within another bank (in another country). End-users can do this by entering the beneficiary account details (note that the BIC can be imported from SWIFT Alliance) or by selecting a registered beneficiary (which stores the beneficiary account details). The payment instruction is submitted in real time and executed by the Core System.

International Transfers

Ruble Transfers

End-users can initiate an international money transfer (using SWIFT or a routine code -  ABA  or Sort code- to identify Beneficiary Bank) from one of their own accounts (or from an account they have the relevant privileges) to a beneficiary held within another bank (in another country). End-users can do this by entering the beneficiary account details (note that the BIC can be imported from SWIFT Alliance) or by selecting a registered beneficiary (which stores the beneficiary account details). The payment instruction is submitted in real time (or can be scheduled for future or repetitive execution) and executed by the Core System.

Know More >>>

End-users can initiate a money transfer in Rubles from one of their own accounts (or from an account they have the relevant privileges) to a beneficiary held in a Russian Bank. End-users can do this by entering the beneficiary account details (note that the BIK code can be imported from a pre-load table) or by selecting a registered beneficiary (which stores the beneficiary account details). The payment instruction is submitted in real time (or can be scheduled for future or repetitive execution) and executed by the Core System.
